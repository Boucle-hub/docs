    ## Displaying your QR code

    Your QR poster lives at **Dashboard → QR Poster** (also called **Card Studio**). It encodes a permanent link to your loyalty card — it never changes, so you only ever need to print it once. Download it as a PNG or print it directly from your browser's print dialog and place it somewhere customers will naturally see it: your counter, your entrance, or your tables.

    ## Approving stamp requests

    Stamps are never added automatically. When a customer scans your QR code and signs in, it creates a **stamp request** with a roughly 30-second window. You or your staff get an instant push notification and approve (or reject) it with one tap in the staff portal — only then does the stamp land on the customer's card. If nobody acts in time, the request simply expires and the customer can try again.

    <Note>
      If you run multiple registers or devices at one location, each device gets its own staff QR variant, so a stamp request routes its push notification to the specific device where the customer is standing — not to every device at once.
    </Note>

    ## Redeeming rewards

    When a customer's card is full, they see a **Redeem** button that generates a 6-digit code (and QR) on their screen — "Show at counter." Staff enter or scan that code in the **Redeem Terminal** (Dashboard → Redeem terminal, or the staff portal on any phone, tablet, or computer). If you've enabled redemption PINs in Settings → Program rules, staff also enter their 6-digit PIN. Once redeemed, the stamp count resets and a new cycle starts — double-redemption isn't possible.

    ## Fraud prevention

    By default, every stamp passes through the same set of checks: the staff-approval gate described above, server-side verification, and a cooldown period between stamps for the same customer (60 minutes by default, adjustable — or disabled — in Settings → Program rules). Together these mean a screenshotted or replayed QR code does nothing on its own; a stamp only ever lands after a live approval.

    <Note>
      Staff approval can be turned off for faster counter service (**Fast Mode**, off by default) — see [Settings](/dashboard/settings#staff-approval-and-fast-mode). With it off, the cooldown still applies, but the approval gate doesn't. Ruffus also watches for other unusual patterns in the background regardless of this setting; see [Settings](/dashboard/settings#fraud-monitoring).
    </Note>

    ## Editing rewards and stamp thresholds

    You can change your stamp count or reward label anytime in Card Studio. Changing the count never resets a customer's existing progress — and if you lower it, customers who newly qualify are granted their reward retroactively.

    ## Analytics and win-back

    Your dashboard overview shows stamps today, member stats, an activity feed, and a close-to-reward view, refreshing about once a minute. The detailed view adds a 7-day chart, a period chart (7/30/90 days), program health, and at-risk members. On the engagement side, Ruffus handles win-back for you automatically: customers inactive for 30\+ days get an automatic win-back email and push (no more than once every 60 days per customer), and you can add a short personal note of up to 280 characters to those emails from Dashboard → Campaigns.

  <Tab title="For Customers">
    ## Collecting a stamp

    Scan the QR code at the counter and sign in once — a phone number or email is enough, no app to download. That creates a stamp request, and staff typically approve it within seconds. Once approved, the stamp appears on your card right away.

    ## Where your card lives

    Your card works straight from your mobile browser — nothing to install. You can also add it to your phone's home screen as a PWA, or add it to Apple Wallet or Google Wallet, available from the moment you first open your card. Wherever you keep it, it updates automatically every time you earn a stamp or redeem a reward.

    ## Getting close to a reward

    Your card shows your progress at a glance, with a friendly nudge once you're close. The moment you complete your card, you'll get an email and push notification letting you know your reward is ready. If it sits unclaimed for two weeks or more, you'll get a reminder so it doesn't get forgotten.

    ## Claiming a reward

    When your card is full, tap **Redeem** to generate a 6-digit code and QR on your screen. Show it to staff at the counter, they'll enter it in their terminal, and your reward is marked redeemed on the spot — then a fresh card starts.

    ## No duplicates, no cheating

    Because every stamp needs a live staff approval and there's a cooldown between stamps, there's no way to rack up stamps by rescanning, screenshotting, or sharing the QR code with someone else. Every stamp on your card reflects a real, staff-confirmed visit.
