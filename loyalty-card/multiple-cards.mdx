Ruffus lets you run several loyalty cards at the same time — a coffee card and a lunch card, a haircut card and a colour card — each with its own name, stamp count, and reward. Cards are managed from **Dashboard → Cards** (owner-only).

## How many cards you can run

| Plan | Active cards |
| --- | --- |
| Basic | Up to 3 |
| Growth | Up to 10 |
| Custom | Unlimited |

A paused card still counts toward your cap — the cap is about how many cards exist, not how many are currently active. Deleting a card frees its slot.

## Creating and editing cards

From **Dashboard → Cards** you can:

- **Create a card** with its own trilingual name (French, English, Spanish), stamp count, and reward label.
- **Edit a card** — change its name, stamp count, or reward at any time. Lowering the stamp count retroactively grants rewards to customers who already qualify, exactly like a single-card program.
- **Pause a card** — it disappears from the customer's view and stops collecting stamps, but nothing is lost (see below).
- **Delete a card** — a soft delete; earned rewards remain redeemable. You can't delete your last card.

<Note>
  Your branding is shared across all cards: the logo, colours, and stamp style you set in **Card Studio** apply to every card. What differs per card is the name, the stamp count, and the reward.
</Note>

## How customers join and stamp

Nothing changes about your QR poster — one poster, one scan:

1. A customer scans your QR and signs in as usual.
2. They're **enrolled in every active card automatically** — no extra steps. Customers who joined earlier are caught up to newly created cards on their next visit.
3. If you run **one** card, the experience is exactly what it's always been. If you run **more than one**, the customer picks which card gets the stamp for this visit before the staff approval goes through.

Staff see which card a stamp request targets in the staff portal whenever more than one card is running, so approvals stay one tap.

## Pausing vs deleting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="What happens when I pause a card?">
    The card stops appearing to customers and stops collecting stamps. Progress and earned rewards are preserved — a customer with a completed card can still redeem their reward while the card is paused. Unpause it and everyone picks up exactly where they left off.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens when I delete a card?">
    Deletion is soft: the card is retired from view, but rewards customers already earned on it remain redeemable at the counter. Stamp progress toward an unfinished reward on a deleted card doesn't carry over to other cards.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What if I downgrade below my card count?">
    When you schedule a downgrade you'll choose which cards stay active; the rest are paused — never deleted — when the new plan takes effect at renewal. Re-upgrading lets you unpause them, with all customer progress intact. See [Plans](/billing/plans) for how downgrades are scheduled.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Campaigns and multiple cards

Bonus-stamp and new-member-bonus campaigns can target a **specific card**, a specific location, or your whole business — so a double-stamp week on the lunch card doesn't touch the coffee card. Set this up from **Dashboard → Campaigns**.

<Tip>
  You often don't need a second card at all. If the goal is a limited-time push — double stamps in March, a bonus for new members — a campaign on your existing card is simpler than a seasonal card, and it ends on schedule automatically.
</Tip>
