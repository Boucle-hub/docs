An NFC station card sits at your counter next to (or instead of) your printed QR poster. A customer taps their phone against it and their stamp lands immediately — no camera, no staff approval step.

<Info>
  NFC cards are optional hardware, included on your plan at no extra cost. How many you can run at once depends on your plan: **1** on Basic, **2** on Growth, **3** on Network. Contact [hello@ruffus.app](mailto:hello@ruffus.app) to order cards for your location.
</Info>

## How a tap works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer taps their phone">
    Holding their phone near the card opens a link in their browser — no app, no camera permission.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ruffus verifies the tap">
    Every tap carries a fresh, single-use cryptographic signature from the card itself. Ruffus checks it's genuine and hasn't been used before, then grants the stamp.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Stamp lands instantly">
    No staff approval needed — the same cooldown and campaign-bonus rules that apply to QR stamps also apply here.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If the customer isn't signed in yet, they're guided through a quick sign-up first, then the stamp is applied automatically once they finish joining — nothing is lost.
</Note>

## Managing your cards

From **Dashboard → QR Poster**, an **NFC Cards** section lists every card enrolled at your location(s), lets you revoke a lost or damaged card, and request more.

- **Revoke** takes effect immediately — a revoked card stops working on its next tap and doesn't count against your plan's card limit, so you can enroll a replacement right away.
- Cards are shipped **pre-programmed** by Ruffus; there's nothing for you to configure on the card itself.

## Compatibility

Most recent phones support tap-to-read out of the box:

- **Android** — any NFC-capable phone with Chrome.
- **iPhone XS and newer** — works with the screen on (the phone can be locked). Older iPhones (7/8 and similar) can't read NFC in the background — those customers use the printed QR poster instead, which always stays active as a fallback.

## What if...

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A customer taps twice in a row?">
    The second tap is treated like any repeat scan — if you have a cooldown set, they'll see a friendly "already stamped" screen rather than earning twice.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone reuses an old tap (screenshot, browser history)?">
    Each tap's signature is single-use. Reopening a previous tap shows a "tap again" screen instead of granting a stamp.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A customer has multiple loyalty cards and taps?">
    If it's clear which of your cards the tap should credit, the stamp is auto-granted. If it's ambiguous, they're shown a quick picker in the app to choose the right card first.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does a tap trigger referral bonuses or points/tier bonuses?">
    Not yet — v1 NFC taps grant the base stamp only. Referral and points/tier bonuses still apply normally to QR-scanned and staff-approved stamps.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can a customer redeem a reward by tapping?">
    Taps are for earning stamps only. Redeeming a completed reward still goes through the usual claim flow — at the Redeem terminal, or via staff PIN approval on the customer's phone if you have that enabled. See [Stamps and Rewards](/loyalty-card/stamps-and-rewards#how-redemption-works).
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
