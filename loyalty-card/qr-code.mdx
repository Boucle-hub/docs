Your QR code is the only thing standing between a customer and their first stamp. Ruffus generates it automatically when you create a card, and it never changes — so a poster you print today will still work a year from now. Getting it in front of customers is the one task that makes everything else run on its own.

<Note>
  Ruffus requires **no POS system, no card reader, and no hardware of any kind**. Your QR code is a printed (or digital) image, and all stamp processing happens in the cloud the moment a customer scans it.
</Note>

## Download Your Printable QR Poster

1. In your Ruffus dashboard, go to **Loyalty Cards** and select your card.
2. Click the **QR Code** tab.
3. Click **Download Poster**. Ruffus generates a print-ready PDF sized for A5 (half-sheet) by default. Use the **Size** dropdown to switch to A4, letter, or a small table-tent format before downloading.
4. Print the poster at home, at a print shop, or send it directly to a commercial printer.

The downloaded file includes your business name, logo, a brief instruction line ("Scan to collect your stamp"), and the QR code itself.

## Where to Display Your QR Code

Place your QR code anywhere customers naturally pause or look during their visit. The more visible it is, the more scans you'll capture.

| Location | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| **Counter or till area** | Customers are already stopped and waiting — perfect moment to scan |
| **Front window** | Catches attention before customers even walk in |
| **Table tent** | Great for cafés and restaurants where customers sit down |
| **Receipt** | Prints alongside the transaction; customers see it at home too |
| **Packaging or bags** | Travels with the customer and prompts a scan later |

You can display the QR code in multiple locations at once — all scans feed into the same card.

## What Happens When a Customer Scans

1. The customer opens their phone's camera (or any QR scanner app) and points it at your code.
2. A link appears — they tap it, and their loyalty card opens **instantly in the browser**. No app download, no account creation, and no waiting.
3. A stamp is added to their card automatically.
4. If it's their first stamp, they see an **Add to Wallet** button to save the card to Apple or Google Wallet.
5. If they're one stamp away from a reward, they receive an automatic reminder.

The whole sequence takes under ten seconds and is entirely self-serve — your staff don't need to touch anything.

## Your QR Code Never Changes

The QR code Ruffus generates for your card is permanent. You can safely:

- Print large-format posters without worrying about reprinting later
- Include the QR code in a permanent window decal or signage
- Add it to printed menus or packaging in bulk

The only reason you would ever need a new QR code is if you **archive a card and create a replacement card** — in that case, a new code is generated for the new card. Your existing printed posters for the old card would stop issuing stamps at that point.

## What If a Customer Can't Scan?

Some customers may have trouble scanning, usually because of an older device, a cracked camera lens, or low light. Here's what to suggest:

- Ask them to try a different QR scanner app (several free options are available on both iOS and Android).
- Move to a brighter spot — QR codes scan more reliably in good light.
- Enlarge the printed poster if possible; codes that are too small can be harder for older cameras to read.

<Note>
  A **manual stamp lookup** feature — allowing staff to issue a stamp by searching for a customer without a scan — is on the Ruffus roadmap. Until it's available, contact support at support@ruffus.app and the team can add a stamp manually after verifying the visit.
</Note>
