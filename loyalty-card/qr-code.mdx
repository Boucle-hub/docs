Your QR poster is the physical bridge between your storefront and your digital loyalty card. It's built automatically from your card's branding — no separate design step.

## Getting your poster

Your poster lives in **Card Studio** at **Dashboard → QR Poster**. It includes your logo, a headline, your reward label, and the QR code itself, all pulled from whatever you've set up in Card Studio.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Download PNG">
    Click **Download** to save the poster as a PNG image. Print it at any size using your own printer or a local print shop, or use it directly in digital signage.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Print from browser">
    Click **Print** to send the poster straight to your browser's print dialog — a quick option if you just need a page for the counter right now.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
  Your QR code encodes a permanent join link that never changes. Print it once and it stays valid for as long as your program is active — no reprinting when you update your branding or reward.
</Note>

## Where to display it

| Location | Why it works |
|---|---|
| Counter or register | Customers see it right as they're paying — the natural moment to scan. |
| Entrance or window | Catches customers on their way in, before they've even ordered. |
| Table tents | Good for sit-down spots where customers have a few idle minutes. |
| Receipts | A QR on a printed or emailed receipt keeps the offer with them after they leave. |

<Tip>
  If you have more than one register or till, set up a device for each in your dashboard and display that device's own QR variant at its register. Each variant routes stamp requests to that specific device's staff push, so whoever is standing at that till gets the approval prompt — not a staff member across the room.
</Tip>

## What happens when a customer scans

<Steps>
  <Step title="Their browser opens the join link">
    No app install. The link opens directly in their phone's camera or browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="They sign in">
    First-time customers create a lightweight account with a phone number (SMS code), email, or Google/Apple sign-in. Returning customers are recognized automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A stamp request goes out">
    The customer sees a live countdown while their request — open for about 30 seconds — waits for staff.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff approves">
    A staff device gets an instant push notification and taps **Approve**. The stamp lands on the card right away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If the customer already earned a stamp recently, they'll see a cooldown screen instead of a new request — this is expected and configurable in **Settings → Program rules**.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting scans

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Customer's camera won't recognize the code">
    Make sure the poster is well-lit and not glared over by overhead lighting or laminate glare. A matte print finish scans more reliably than glossy.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer can't get their camera app to open a link">
    Older phones sometimes need a dedicated QR scanner app rather than the native camera. As a fallback, they can also open the join link directly — the same URL your poster encodes works fine typed or tapped from anywhere, including a link on your website or social bio.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer says they scanned but nothing happened for staff">
    Check the staff device — the approval screen shows pending requests the instant they come in, so if nothing appeared the scan likely didn't complete, or the request already expired after 30 seconds. Have the customer try again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Multiple staff devices, wrong one got the notification">
    This usually means the wrong QR variant is on display. Confirm each register is showing its own per-device QR, not a shared one.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
