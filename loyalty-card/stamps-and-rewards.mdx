Every stamp in Ruffus starts as a request from the customer and only lands once a staff member approves it. That one-tap approval is the whole fraud-prevention model — it's what keeps rewards going only to real visits, not a limitation to work around.

## How a customer earns a stamp

<Steps>
  <Step title="Customer scans the QR code">
    They point their phone camera at your poster and open the join link in their browser. No app to download.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customer signs in">
    On their first visit, they create a lightweight account with a phone number (SMS code), email, or Google/Apple sign-in. Returning customers are recognized automatically. No app download — everything happens in the browser.
  </Step>
  <Step title="A stamp request is created">
    The customer sees a live countdown. This request stays open for about 30 seconds.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff gets a push notification">
    Whoever is holding the staff device — phone, tablet, or the counter computer — gets an instant push notification with the pending request.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff approves in one tap">
    Staff taps **Approve**. The stamp lands on the customer's card immediately and their screen updates in real time. Staff can also **Reject** a request that doesn't look right.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  If staff don't act within the ~30-second window, the request simply expires. The customer can scan again to start a new one — nothing is lost.
</Note>

## Cooldown between stamps

To prevent the same visit from generating multiple stamps, Ruffus enforces a cooldown after each stamp is earned — 60 minutes by default. Merchants can adjust or disable this in **Settings → Program rules**. If a customer scans again before the cooldown ends, they see a friendly screen letting them know when they can earn their next stamp.

## How redemption works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Card fills up">
    Once a customer reaches the required stamp count, a **Redeem** button appears on their card.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Customer generates a code">
    Tapping Redeem shows a 6-digit code and a QR code on their screen, labeled "Show at counter."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Staff enters the code">
    At **Dashboard → Redeem terminal** (or the staff portal on any phone, tablet, or computer), staff type in or scan that code. If the merchant has redemption PINs enabled, staff also enter their 6-digit PIN.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reward is marked redeemed">
    The stamp count resets and a new cycle begins right away. The code is single-use, so the same reward can never be redeemed twice.
  </Step>
</Steps>

You can review redemption activity anytime at **Dashboard → Rewards**, which shows KPIs, full redemption history, and your top redeemers.

<Tip>
  Want to reward by dollars spent or specific products instead of just stamp count? See [Points](/loyalty-card/points) — an optional layer you can run alongside your stamp card.
</Tip>

## Reward expiry (optional)

By default, an earned reward never expires. From **Settings → Program rules**, you can optionally set an expiry window (7 to 365 days) after which an unredeemed reward becomes void — Ruffus automatically reminds the customer before it does. See [Campaigns & Automated Messages](/dashboard/campaigns#reward-expiry-and-expiration-reminders) for how the reminder works.

## Editing your reward

You can rename or re-describe your reward — including updating the French, English, and Spanish labels — at any time from Card Studio. Editing a reward's name or description never affects customer progress; everyone keeps the stamps they've already earned.

## Keeping stamps honest

Ruffus doesn't rely on tricks like hidden watermarks or cryptographic scan signatures to catch fraud — it relies on a simpler, sturdier model:

- Every stamp requires a staff member to approve it, in person, in the moment.
- The cooldown window prevents rapid repeat scans from the same visit.
- Stamp requests are verified server-side and expire automatically if unapproved.
- A screenshot or a replay of the QR code does nothing on its own — it still has to go through a live approval from staff holding the device.

This does mean staff have a small role to play: one tap per stamp. In exchange, every stamp on every card reflects a real visit.

## What if...

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="A customer loses their progress?">
    Progress is tied to their account, not their device. If they sign in again — even on a new phone — their stamp history and current progress come right back. If they've added the card to Apple or Google Wallet, that pass also keeps their progress. If something still looks wrong, they (or you, on their behalf) can reach out to [hello@ruffus.app](mailto:hello@ruffus.app).
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A customer tries to redeem the same reward twice?">
    It isn't possible. Each redemption code is single-use — once it's been entered at the terminal, it can't be used again.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A stamp didn't register after a scan?">
    The most common causes are the cooldown window (they may need to wait before their next stamp is eligible) or the 30-second approval window expiring before staff could act. Have them scan again to start a fresh request.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="A reward gets redeemed by mistake?">
    There's currently no self-serve way to reverse a redemption. Contact [hello@ruffus.app](mailto:hello@ruffus.app) and the team can help sort it out.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
