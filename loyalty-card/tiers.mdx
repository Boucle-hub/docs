Every card can turn on VIP tiers — a ladder of loyalty levels that reward your best customers automatically as they rack up lifetime stamps or points, no separate campaign needed.

<Note>
  Tiers are available on every plan — they aren't gated behind a specific tier of Ruffus itself. Currently, tiers work on shared cards only; a card running in **granular mode** across multiple locations (see [Multi-Location](/dashboard/multi-location)) can't configure tiers yet.
</Note>

## Turning on tiers

From **Dashboard → Tiers**, switch tiers on for a card. Once enabled, you build a ladder of levels — there's no cap on how many you can add.

## Building your ladder

Each tier has:

- **A name**, written in French, English, and Spanish — the customer sees the version matching their language, same as reward labels.
- **A threshold** — the lifetime total a customer needs to reach to enter that tier. Thresholds are based on whichever metric your card already tracks: lifetime stamps, or lifetime points if [Points](/loyalty-card/points) is turned on.
- **A bonus-stamp perk** — extra stamps (up to 20) added automatically every time a customer in that tier earns a stamp. It's the one lever tiers currently offer — there's no separate multiplier or discount configuration.

Tiers are ordered automatically by threshold, lowest to highest, so a customer always sits in the highest tier they qualify for.

## What customers see

Once a customer crosses a tier's threshold, their tier name appears as a badge on their card in the customer portal and on their Apple or Google Wallet pass — updating automatically the same way stamp counts do. There's no separate "tiers" screen; it's a label layered on top of the card they already have.

## Progress is never lost

Because tiers are based on lifetime totals, a customer never drops out of a tier they've already reached — even after redeeming a reward and starting a fresh stamp cycle, their lifetime total (and tier) stays put.

<Tip>
  Tiers work well alongside [Campaigns](/dashboard/campaigns) — for example, use a milestone campaign to celebrate a customer entering a new tier, on top of the bonus stamps the tier itself grants.
</Tip>
